BIBLICAL STUDIES (BBL)

The major component in this division is the bible. It aims at building strong biblical foundation upon the student's education. The following are the courses under this division:


BBL 211 · GENESIS (3CR)

Course Description :

This book focuses on exposition of acts of God and the beginning of human government.

Content :

The understanding of its authorship, purpose of writing, themes of the book, outline, theological doctrine versus world view and the lessons from the book for the contemporary church.



BBL 112 · GENESIS-RUTH (3CR)

Course Description :

Focuses on history, Cultural background and God's will for humanity.

Content :

The structure of the book, authorship, themes, purposes and message application.



BBL 123 · ISAMUEL-SONGS OF SOLOMON (3CR)

Course Description :

The course focuses on history, cultural background and God's will for humanity.

Content :

Deals with Authorship, dates, Historical backgrounds, Themes and Application for each book for today.



BBL 134 · ISAIAH TO MALACHI (3CR)

Course Description :

Focuses on history cultural background and God's will for humanity.

Content :

The structure of the book, authorship, Date, historical backgrounds themes and application for each book for today.



BBL 215 · ROMANS (3CR)

Course Description :

The book focuses on exposition of the book.

Content :

Authorship, Date, theme, Purpose, outline, theological doctrines in relation to contemporary world view and application for the church.



BBL 226 · REVELATION (3CR)

Course Description :

Focuses on the exposition of apocalyptic book majoring on eschatological events.

Content :

Authorship, date, theme, purpose, Outline, Various interpretations theological doctrines in relation to contemporary worldview, eschatological arrangements and application for the church.



BBL 237 · GOSPEL AND ACTS (3CR)

Course Description :

The course focuses on the study of the four gospels and the Acts of the Apostles as a foundation of the church.

Content :

Authorship, Dates, Historical background, various interpretations, themes, and the application of the book for today.



BBL 328 · HEBREWS (3CR)

Course Description :

The study focuses on the exposition of the book.

Content :

Authorship, Date, Themes, Purposes various interpretations and the application of the book for today.



BBL 339 · GENERAL EPISTLES (3CR)

Course Description :

Focuses on the study of general epistles e.g. James, 1 & 2 Peter, 1, 2 & 3 John and Jude.

Content :

Authorship, historical backgrounds, themes, purposes, outlines and application for today.
