EDUCATIONAL STUDIES (EDU)

This division seeks to provide historical and biblical foundations, teaching ministry undertaken by the church in order to communicate effectively the principles of Christian beliefs. The division provides the following courses:-


EDU 131 · BIBLE STUDY METHODS - (2CR)

Course Description :

The course focuses on the theory and the practice of inductive Bible Study methods.

Content :

The foundations and tools of Bible study, character or biological method, topical or theme method, historical method and word study method.



EDU 122 · CHRISTIAN EDUCATION 1 (2CR)

Course Description :

The course focuses on the principles and practices of Christian education in the local church.

Content :

Definitions, history and philosophy of Christian Education, importance of Christian education, lesson planning characteristics of a learner and dynamics of teaching or learning process.



EDU 133 · CHRISTIAN EDUCATION 11 (2CR)

Course Description :

The course focuses on organization of ministries within the local church.

Content :

Goals in Christian education evaluating existing programmes and implementing new ones, organization and integration and personal development.



EDU 134 · 1NTRODUCTION TO SOCIOLOGY - (3CR)

Course Description :

The course focuses on the study of people and how they interact with each other and the various social groups.

Content :

Definitions, importance of sociology, cultural and diversity, socialization, social interaction and social structure groups and organizations, deviance, crime and social control. Social stratification and social classes.



EDU 135 · INTRODUCTION TO PHILOSOPHY (3CR)

Course Description :

The course will examine different philosophical theories, comparing and contrasting them as we apply their views to different philosophical questions.

Content :

Definition, general consideration of the human nature and the nature of the universe, knowledge, perception, freedom and determinism. The existence of God, the origin of philosophy, types of philosophies and key people in philosophy. Philosophical study of reasoning, the function and use of language, distinction between deductive and inductive arguments, methods for symbolizing and evaluating the validity of arguments and the detection of informal fallacies.



EDU 216 · PRINCIPLES AND METHODS OF TEACHING (3CR)

Course Description :

The course is designed to develop students' knowledge and skills for effective teaching.

Content :

Definitions, characteristics of learners, syllabus, schemes of work, lessons plan, methods of teaching, assessments of students, class control.



EDU 117 · BIBLE HISTORY AND GEOGRAPHY (2CR)

Course Description :

The course deals with historical and geographical sites of the Bible

Content :

Important historical, sites like Haran, Canaan, Eden and Paul's missionary journeys. The relevance of historical site to the church today.



EDU 318 · CHURCH AND MUSIC (3CR)

Course Description :

The course focuses on general music theory and literacy and Combining the use of scripture and related literature in developing sacred music.

Content :

Definition, the role and importance of music in church service, worship leading, choir development, solfa and staff notations, key and time signatures, intervals chords and triads.



EDU 339 · INTRODUCTION TO PSYCHOLOGY (2CR)

Course Description :

This course provides an introduction to concepts and theories of psychology and their application to real life situation.

Content :

Definitions, sensation and perception, state of consciousness, learning, memory, motivation and emotion, theories, health, stress and coping. Application of psychological concepts and principles, Human behavior and mental processes.
